# Why We Built a Free Quilt Design Tool

When we started QuiltCorgi, one of the first decisions we made was that the core design experience would be free. Not a limited trial, not a stripped-down demo — a genuinely useful tool that lets you design real quilts without ever entering a credit card.

## The Problem with Quilting Software

We surveyed dozens of quilters before writing a single line of code. A clear pattern emerged:

- Most quilting software costs between $100 and $250 for a desktop license
- Some require annual subscriptions of $60-120 per year
- Many quilters use graph paper or free tools like coloring book printouts because the software is too expensive
- Beginners are especially price-sensitive and often give up before discovering how rewarding digital design can be

This pricing model made sense in the era of shrink-wrapped software sold in quilt shops. But the web changed everything. The cost of serving one more user in a browser-based tool is measured in fractions of a penny. We saw an opportunity to rethink the economics.

## Our Free Tier Philosophy

QuiltCorgi's free tier is designed to be genuinely useful, not frustrating:

### What Is Free

- The complete block library (all 659+ blocks)
- All six layout types
- EasyDraw block drafting
- Applique design tools
- Color and fabric assignment
- PDF export with actual-size templates
- Community browsing and sharing
- Up to 3 saved projects
- Up to 3 design variations per project

### What Pro Adds

- Unlimited projects and variations
- Advanced export formats (FPP, rotary cutting charts)
- Batch operations and bulk recoloring
- Priority email support
- Early access to new features

The boundary is drawn at productivity and scale, not capability. A quilter on the free tier can design and produce a complete quilt from start to finish. Pro is for quilters who design frequently and want workflow efficiency.

## Why This Matters

Quilting has historically been one of the most accessible crafts. People have made quilts from scraps, using techniques passed down through generations, with minimal equipment. We believe the design tools should reflect that tradition of accessibility.

When a beginner quilter has an idea for a design, the path from idea to pattern should have as few barriers as possible. If cost is the first barrier they encounter, many will never get past it. And quilting loses a potential lifelong enthusiast.

## How We Sustain the Project

A reasonable question: if the core tool is free, how does QuiltCorgi survive?

Our sustainability model has three pillars:

### 1. Pro Subscriptions

Serious quilters who design regularly find genuine value in the Pro tier's unlimited projects, advanced exports, and workflow tools. The subscription is priced affordably relative to fabric costs (a single yard of quilting cotton costs more than a month of Pro).

### 2. Community Marketplace (Coming Soon)

We are building a marketplace where pattern designers can sell their QuiltCorgi designs. We take a small commission on sales. This creates a virtuous cycle: more designers attract more users, which attracts more designers.

### 3. Efficient Engineering

By building on modern web technologies and keeping our infrastructure lean, our per-user costs are remarkably low. We do not need a massive user base to break even. This removes the pressure to monetize aggressively.

## The Broader Vision

We see QuiltCorgi as part of a larger trend toward democratizing creative tools. Just as Canva made graphic design accessible, Figma made interface design collaborative, and Tinkercad made 3D modeling approachable, we want to make quilt design something anyone can try.

The quilting community is extraordinarily generous with knowledge, patterns, and encouragement. Our tool should embody that same generosity.
