# Using Generators: Symmetry & Serendipity

QuiltCorgi's generator tools help you discover designs you might never create manually. By combining symmetry operations with randomized color assignments, you can explore thousands of variations in minutes.

## What Are Generators?

Generators are automated tools that transform your quilt design based on mathematical rules. They work on two levels:

- **Symmetry generators** — Apply mirror, rotation, and reflection operations to your block layout
- **Color generators** — Randomly assign colors or fabrics to patches based on constraints you define

Together, they create the "serendipity" factor — happy accidents that lead to stunning designs.

## Mirror Modes

Mirror modes duplicate and reflect sections of your quilt to create symmetrical patterns. Access them from **Layout Settings > Symmetry** in the context panel.

### Vertical Mirror

Copies the left half of your quilt to the right, flipped horizontally. Great for:

- Balanced traditional quilts
- Ensuring left-right symmetry in pictorial designs

### Horizontal Mirror

Copies the top half to the bottom, flipped vertically. Useful for:

- Top-to-bottom balanced layouts
- Landscape-style quilts with sky/ground symmetry

### Quadrant Mirror

Copies the top-left quadrant to all four quadrants with appropriate reflections. This creates:

- Classic medallion-like symmetry from a single corner design
- Intricate patterns from surprisingly simple starting arrangements

> **Tip:** Design just one quadrant of your quilt with unique blocks and colors, then apply quadrant mirror. The result is a complex-looking quilt that only required a quarter of the design work.

### Diagonal Mirror

Reflects along the diagonal axis, creating designs where the top-left triangle mirrors the bottom-right. This produces unexpected and often striking results.

## Radial Symmetry

Radial symmetry rotates a wedge-shaped section around a center point. This is the basis of kaleidoscope quilts and is available in the **Generators** toolbar.

1. Select **Radial Symmetry** from the generators menu
2. Choose the number of segments: 4, 6, 8, 12, or 16
3. Design the first wedge — any blocks, colors, or fabrics you place are replicated around the center
4. Adjust the rotation offset to fine-tune the alignment

### Kaleidoscope Effect

Combine radial symmetry with [fussy cutting](/tutorials/fabric-fussy-cutting) for stunning kaleidoscope quilts:

1. Upload a fabric with a bold print
2. Fussy cut one wedge to capture a specific motif
3. Apply 8-segment radial symmetry
4. The motif repeats and mirrors into a complex mandala pattern

## Random Color Generation

The **Randomize Colors** generator assigns random colors to patches based on rules you define.

### Basic Random

Click **Randomize** to assign completely random colors from your active palette. Each click generates a new variation.

### Constrained Random

For more controlled results, set constraints:

- **Palette lock** — Only use colors from a specific palette (e.g., your fabric collection's available colors)
- **Patch grouping** — Keep patches of the same template in the same color across the quilt
- **Contrast minimum** — Ensure adjacent patches have sufficient contrast for visual definition
- **Color count** — Limit to a specific number of unique colors (e.g., "use only 4 colors")

### Seed Values

Every random generation uses a seed value. If you find a variation you love:

1. Note the seed number displayed in the generators panel
2. You can re-enter that seed later to reproduce the exact same result
3. Share seed values with other quilters who have the same block layout

> **Tip:** Increment a seed value by 1 to see a similar-but-different variation. Seeds that are close numerically tend to produce related color arrangements.

## Combining Generators

The real power comes from combining generators. Try this workflow:

1. Place blocks in a simple grid layout
2. Apply **quadrant mirror** for instant symmetry
3. Run **random color generation** with a 5-color palette
4. Click **Randomize** several times to explore variations
5. When you find something promising, fine-tune individual patches manually

## Saving Variations

Each generator result can be saved as a **Design Variation**. Your free account stores 3 variations per project; Pro accounts get unlimited. This lets you compare different generator results side by side before committing to one.
