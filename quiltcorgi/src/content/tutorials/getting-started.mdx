# Getting Started: Your First Quilt Design

Welcome to QuiltCorgi! This tutorial walks you through creating your very first quilt design, from signing up to saving a finished project. No prior experience required.

## Step 1: Create Your Account

Head to [quiltcorgi.com](/auth/signup) and click **Get Started Free**. You can sign up with your email address or use Google sign-in. The free tier gives you access to the full block library and basic design tools — no credit card needed.

> **Tip:** Your free account includes up to 3 saved projects and 3 design variations per project. Upgrade to Pro anytime for unlimited projects and variations.

## Step 2: Open the Studio

After signing in, click **Open Studio** from your dashboard. The studio is where all the design magic happens. You will see four main areas:

- **Top Bar** — Switch between worktables (Quilt, Block, Image, Print) using the segmented tab control
- **Tool Rail** — The vertical icon strip on the left with your drawing and selection tools
- **Canvas** — The large central area where your quilt design lives
- **Context Panel** — The right-side panel showing properties, colors, and settings for your current selection

## Step 3: Choose a Layout

Your new project starts with a default grid layout. To change it, open the **Layout Settings** in the context panel. QuiltCorgi supports several layout types:

1. **Grid** — Traditional rows and columns of blocks
2. **On-Point** — Blocks rotated 45 degrees for a diamond effect
3. **Sashing** — Grid with strips between blocks
4. **Medallion** — Concentric rounds emanating from a center block
5. **Lone Star** — The classic eight-pointed star radiating from center

For your first project, stick with **Grid**. Set the number of rows and columns — a 4x4 grid is a great starting size for a throw quilt.

> **See also:** [Layout Types: Grid, On-Point, Medallion & Lone Star](/tutorials/layout-types) for a deep dive on all layout options.

## Step 4: Add Your First Block

Click the **Block Library** button in the tool rail to browse the library of 659+ quilt blocks across 20+ categories. You can search by name, browse by category (stars, log cabins, pinwheels, pictorial, and more), or filter by complexity.

To add a block to your quilt:

1. Find a block you like — try searching for "Ohio Star" or browsing the Stars category
2. Click the block thumbnail to preview it
3. Drag the block onto a cell in your grid, or double-click to fill the selected cell

Repeat this process to fill your grid. You can use the same block in every cell for a unified look, or mix different blocks for variety.

## Step 5: Assign Colors and Fabrics

Select any block on the canvas, then use the **Color Palette** in the context panel to assign colors to each patch. Click a patch in the block, then choose a color from the palette or enter a hex value.

For a more realistic preview, switch to **Fabric Mode** by uploading your own fabric images. See the [Fabric Management & Fussy Cutting](/tutorials/fabric-fussy-cutting) tutorial for details.

> **Tip:** Use the Colorway tool (the paint bucket icon) to quickly recolor your entire quilt with a different palette. It is a great way to explore variations without changing each patch individually.

## Step 6: Save Your Project

Click the **Save** button in the top bar (or press `Ctrl+S` / `Cmd+S`). Give your project a name and an optional description. Your project saves to the cloud automatically after the first manual save.

You can access all your saved projects from the **Dashboard** at any time.
