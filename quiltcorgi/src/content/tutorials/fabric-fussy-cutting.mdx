# Fabric Management & Fussy Cutting

One of QuiltCorgi's most powerful features is the ability to upload real fabric images and see exactly how they will look on your quilt. Fussy cutting takes this further — letting you position specific motifs precisely on individual patches.

## Uploading Fabric Images

Open the **Fabric Panel** from the context panel sidebar. Click **Upload Fabric** and select an image of your fabric. For best results:

- Photograph or scan a flat fabric swatch with even lighting
- Aim for at least 300 DPI resolution
- Capture enough of the pattern to show at least one full repeat
- Keep the image square or rectangular with pattern aligned to edges

Uploaded fabrics appear in your personal fabric library and persist across projects.

## Step 1: Calibrate Your Fabric

After uploading, QuiltCorgi prompts you to calibrate the fabric scale. This ensures the on-screen fabric matches real-world dimensions.

1. Click **Calibrate** on the uploaded fabric thumbnail
2. Draw a line across a known measurement in the fabric image (for example, the width of a pattern repeat)
3. Enter the real-world measurement (e.g., "6 inches")
4. QuiltCorgi calculates the correct pixels-per-inch ratio

> **Tip:** If you know the manufacturer's pattern repeat size, enter that directly in the calibration dialog without drawing a line.

Accurate calibration is critical for fussy cutting and for printed templates to match your actual fabric.

## Step 2: Assign Fabric to Patches

Select a block on your quilt canvas, then select a patch within the block. In the context panel, click the **Fabric** tab and choose one of your uploaded fabrics. The patch immediately shows a realistic preview of that fabric.

You can assign different fabrics to different patches within the same block. QuiltCorgi tracks all fabric assignments and includes them in your cutting list.

## Step 3: Enable Fussy Cutting

Fussy cutting means deliberately positioning a fabric's motif on a specific patch — centering a flower, aligning a stripe, or capturing a particular section of the print.

To fussy cut a patch:

1. Select the patch that has a fabric assigned
2. Click the **Fussy Cut** button in the context panel (scissors icon)
3. The Fussy Cut Dialog opens, showing your full fabric image with the patch shape overlaid

## Step 4: Position the Motif

In the Fussy Cut Dialog:

- **Drag** the fabric image to slide it behind the patch shape
- **Scroll** to zoom in/out for precision placement
- **Rotate** the fabric if you want a motif at an angle (use the rotation slider)
- **Scale** for fine adjustment if your calibration needs a tweak

The patch outline stays fixed while the fabric moves underneath, so you can see exactly what will be visible in the final quilt.

## Step 5: Pattern Repeat Mode

For fabrics with regular repeats (like geometric prints or florals), enable **Pattern Repeat** mode in the Fussy Cut Dialog. This tiles the fabric image seamlessly so you can:

- See how the repeat aligns across adjacent patches
- Ensure consistent motif placement when the same fabric appears in multiple patches
- Identify the optimal offset for centering motifs

> **See also:** [Using Generators: Symmetry & Serendipity](/tutorials/generators) for auto-rotating fussy cut positions to create kaleidoscope effects.

## Fabric Library Management

Your fabric library supports:

- **Folders** — Organize fabrics by collection, color family, or project
- **Tags** — Add searchable tags like "floral," "batik," or "holiday"
- **Favorites** — Star frequently-used fabrics for quick access
- **Delete** — Remove fabrics you no longer need (assignments in existing projects are preserved)

## Fussy Cut Metadata

Each fussy cut stores metadata per patch: fabric ID, X/Y offset, rotation, and scale. This data persists with your project and exports to your cutting list with precise positioning instructions.
